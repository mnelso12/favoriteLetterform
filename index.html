The original Google font, "Lato", was designed in the summer of 2010 by Warsaw-based designer ≈Åukasz Dziedzic. Originally intended for corporate use, Lato was created to show originality in large text sizes, yet professional and standard for large blocks of text. Lato was named after it's summery, youthful feel, for Lato means "Summer" in Polish.

The semi-rounded glyphs several complementary attributes, exhibiting strength and feeling, stability and seriousness. Classical proportions, as described by Google, provide the characters with harmony and elegance. Lato is currently featured in over six million websites, including that of the University of Notre Dame's very own trumpet section. Traditional, yet modern and bright, Lato is the epitome of open-source font-faces.
